Security, explained without the jargon

ChatGuard inspects the live-chat widgets on a website and checks whether private conversations are properly locked. Here is what that means — in plain language.

The two problems we look for

Digital Impersonation

Technical name: IDOR

Imagine a library where your books sit on a private shelf. The shelves are simply numbered 1, 2, 3… With no guard at the door, anyone can walk up, guess your shelf number, and pull your book straight off it.

Online, those “shelf numbers” are user IDs. If the chat system never checks who is asking, a stranger can read another person’s messages just by changing a number.

The Missing Seal

Technical name: Missing HMAC

Sending a message should be like mailing a secret letter inside a sealed envelope. The wax seal proves it is really from you and that nobody tampered with it along the way.

Many chat widgets ship the letter with no envelope at all. ChatGuard checks whether that protective “seal” (a signature called HMAC) is present — or broken.

Why It Matters

The bottom line

A single missing check can expose thousands of private conversations — and the people running the website often have no idea it is happening.

ChatGuard finds these gaps the way an attacker would, so they can be fixed before anyone’s data is exposed.

How ChatGuard works — in three steps
1

Target

You provide a website address. That’s the only thing we need to begin.

2

Analyze

ChatGuard hunts through the page for the hidden “App IDs” that power the chat widget.

3

Verify

We check whether the “Digital Lock” (HMAC) is actually switched on — or wide open.

Potential Risk

What’s at stake when these checks are missing

Without the lock and the seal in place, private conversations become readable by anyone who knows where to look. That commonly includes:

✉️ Email addresses
📞 Phone numbers
🔑 Account backup codes
💬 Full message history

ChatGuard surfaces these gaps early — turning an invisible, silent exposure into a clear, fixable finding.
